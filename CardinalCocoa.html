Mechanical Instant Coffee Portioner

I worked as part of a team of four to develop an entirely mechanical process for quickly portioning powdered coffee for sale by volume. The designed consisted two butterfly valves seperated by a fixed volume that would fill with coffee. There was a hopper for excess coffee mounted to the top of the device.

The animation shows how an operator would use the device. The handle would be lifted up opening the top valve. This would allow powdered coffee to fill the fixed volume. As you lowered the handle, the top butterfly valve would close partitioning the fixed volume from the hopper. As the handle reached the bottom of the device, the bottom butterfly valve would open, allowing the coffee to fill the jars the powder would be sold in.

To test the effectiveness of the device, we created a working prototype using 80/20 extrusion and a mill. The group then presented the working prototype and engineering drawings for the device to a high school student run machine shop in Wisconsin. The group would then be able to replicate and sell the device to their customer.
